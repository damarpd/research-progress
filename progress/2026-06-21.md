#### Overview

Pedestrian logic defines the rules and processes governing how simulated passengers move within the simulation environment. Passengers are categorized into three types — **alighting**, **boarding**, and **staying** — each represented as a circle with a 0.4 m diameter (a standard proxy for body size). Each passenger type follows its own distinct logic to carry out the corresponding behavior. Full program flowcharts implemented in AnyLogic are provided in Appendix A of the paper.

---

#### 3.3.1 Pedestrian Logic of Alighting Passengers

Alighting passengers select the **nearest door** and move to the corresponding interior waiting area inside the carriage. They queue and wait there until the door opens. Once the door opens, all alighting passengers pass through the door line and exit onto the platform, where they proceed to the **platform sink line** and are removed from the simulation.

---

#### 3.3.2 Pedestrian Logic of Boarding Passengers

Boarding passengers first move to the **platform waiting lines closest to their intended boarding door**. Each waiting line has a maximum capacity of **3 passengers**; additional passengers queue in the wider platform waiting areas. The boarding process is sequenced after alighting: boarding passengers only begin to enter the carriage through the door line once the number of remaining alighting passengers in the corresponding interior zone drops to **N_r or fewer**. After boarding, passengers wait inside the corresponding interior waiting area.

---

#### 3.3.3 Pedestrian Logic of Staying Passengers

Staying passengers are generated at the **interior source line** of the carriage. If seats are available, they occupy them in order until all seats are filled. Remaining standing passengers choose random locations inside the carriage and stand there. When alighting or boarding passengers move nearby, staying passengers **slightly shift their position** to yield space to the moving passengers.

---

#### Key Takeaways

- Passenger movement is governed by type-specific rules that reflect real-world queuing and yielding behavior.
- The boarding process is explicitly **conditioned on the alighting process** via a threshold parameter N_r, capturing the "alight-first, board-second" norm.
- Staying passengers play a passive but interactive role, dynamically adjusting positions to accommodate passenger flow.
- The model is implemented in **AnyLogic** using the Social Force Model (SFM) as the underlying movement engine.
