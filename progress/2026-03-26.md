## LitRev on Social force model for pedestrian dynamics
- author: Dirk Helbing and Peter Molnar
- publication year: 1995
  
### Background
- Social-fields or social forces could lead to behavioral changes by Lewin (1951)
- This idea (social fields or social forces) would be applied to pedestrian behavior according to this paper

### Key findings
- This paper proposed social force model in the context of pedestrian motion and consisted in three rules:
1. Pedestrian wants to reach a certain destination as comfortable as possible by choosing the shortest possible way (without e.g., detours) -> leading to <mark>acceleration term</mark>:

$$\hat{F}_\alpha^0(\hat{v}_\alpha, {v}_\alpha^0\hat{e}_\alpha)$$

2. The motion of pedestrian is influenced by other pedestrians $(\beta)$ and borders ${B}$ such as buildings, walls, street, obstacles, etc. -> leading to keep a certain distance since, normally, feeling uncomfortable when stands closer distance -> <mark>repulsive effect</mark> towards other pedestrians and border -> and this is monotonic decreasing

Other pedestrians:

$$\hat{F}_{\alpha\beta}(\hat{e}_\alpha,\hat{r}_\alpha-\hat{r}_\beta)$$

Borders: 

$$\hat{F}_{\alpha B}(\hat{r}_{\alpha B})$$

3. Pedestrian are sometimes attracted by other persons (e.g., friends, street artist, etc.) or objects (e.g., window displays) -> leading to <mark> attractive effects</mark>

$$\hat{F}_{\alpha i}(\hat{e}_\alpha,\hat{r}_\alpha-\hat{r}_{i}, t)$$

Note:
The repulsive and attractive effects on pedestrian’s behavior have considered the effective angle $2\gamma$ of sight (desired direction and behind pedestrian)

The equation of pedestrian's total motivation $\hat{F}_{\alpha}(t)$:

$$\hat{F}_\alpha(t) = \hat{F}_{\alpha}^0+\sum_{\beta}\hat{F}_{\alpha\beta}+\sum_{B}\hat{F}_{\alpha B}+\sum_{i}\hat{F}_{\alpha i}$$

The social force model is defined by:

$$\frac{d\hat{w}_\alpha}{dt}=\hat{F}_\alpha(t)+fluctuations$$
