## Recent update on social force model

### Exploring the complexity of pedestrian dynamics: impact of societal behavior and personal attributes in urban environments
- **Rationale**:
- This study aims to investigate pedestrian dynamics by focusing on the impact of social and cultural factors, such as dress codes and gender at the operational decision-making level, and explores how these factors, along with societal norms, values, and practices, shape pedestrian behavior in various facilities.
- Focused on real-world pedestrian facilities and experimental context in Iran.
- **Data collection**: Data collection was conducted in sidewalks and signalized and unsignalized intersections, and during evacuation drill in Iran.
- Pedestrian movement trajectories were capture through video recordings.
- This study proposed Culturally Informed Social Force Model (CISFM), incorporating cultural and social parameters.

### Modelling negative interactions among pedestrians in high density situations

- Study was conducted by [Bandini, S., et.al., (2014)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2013.12.007)
- In a specific Section 2 Pedestrians and crowd modelling approaches, [Bandini, S., et.al., (2014)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2013.12.007) specified microscopics pedestrian models that can be organised into broad classes identified according to the way pedestrians are represented and managed, and in particular: (i) pedestrians as *particles subject to forces* of attraction/repulsion, (ii) pedestrians as particular *states of cells* in a CA, (iii) pedestrians as *autonomous agents*, situated in an environment.
