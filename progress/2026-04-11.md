## Recent update (the state-of-art) of the social force model
### Social force models for pedestrian traffic state-of-art
- [Chen, Xu, et.al, 2017](https://doi.org/10.1080/01441647.2017.1396265) conducted comprehensive review on recent social force model by emphasizing summary of the existing researches and new insight for further research.
> Pedestrian microcopic simulation models can be divided into continuous and discrete models.
- Above study developed framework (assessment criteria) for pedestrian models focussing on pedestrian attributes crowd and individual, motion base cases, self-organisation phenomena, and some special ceses.
- Pedestrian attributes are inputs of pedestrian dynamics model, which can be devided into physical attributes and social attributes. 
- Physical attributes include age, height, shape (i.e. circle, ellipse or other), gender, ability, temper, visual range, mass, and walking speed.
- Social attributes include profession, aim, familiarity, luggage, and group.
- Social force model consider shape, mass, walking speed, and group as pedestrian attributes.
- Motion base cases are the basic movement forms that the pedestrian shows in the environment.
> Based on previous study, this study argued that self-organisation phenomenon is a  result of non-linear interactions between many objects or subjects without the intervention of external influences, and it often causes different kinds of spatial-temporal patterns of motion.
- This study also mentioned the update of the original SFM -> attraction forces/attractive effect ($\hat{F}_{\alpha i}$), fluctuation term and direction dependence have been taken out from the original SFM.
- Based on the comprehensive review, this study identified **improved original SFM** by four aspects. (1) pedestrian attributes; (2) motion base cases; (3) self-organisation phenomenon; (4) special cases, explained in below table.

### State-of-art crowd motion simulation models
- [Duives D.C.,Daamen W., Hoogendoorn S.P., 2013](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2013.02.005) conducted study that aims to assess the current pedestrian simulation models with respect to the movement of crowds (*not individual pedestrian movement*) in order to show the differences between the modeling approaches and to indicate whether gaps in the field of crowd modeling research exist.
- Quantitively, the authors proposed the crowd by the definition:
> A crowd is a large group of individuals $N > 100 P$ within the same space at the same time whose movements are for a prolonged period of time $(t >= 60 s)$ dependent on predominantly local interactions $(k > 1 P/m^2)$ 
- In this study behavioral assesment was carried out resulting -> (1) motion base cases and (2) crowd movement phenomena.
- Motion base cases: (a) uni-directional flow, (b) uni-directional rounding a corner, (c) uni-directional-entering, (d) uni-directional exiting, (e) bi-directional flows, (f) crossing flows-2 flows, (g) crossing flows-more than 2 flows, (h) crossing flows-random. 
> Self-organization is defined as the spontaneous establishment of qualitatively new behavior through the non-linear interaction of many objects or subjects without intervention of external influences. 
- Aggregate pedestrian movements/phenomena/characteristics of the crowds: (a) lane formation; (b) Stop and Go; (c) turbulance (d) herding; (e) the zipper effect; (f) faster-is-slower effect.
- In addition to behavioral aspect, the assessment of model applicability was caried out comprising following category ->

|Category|Definition|Relation to study|
|--------|----------|-----------------|
|Strategic process| Pedestrian motion is not only influenced by physical properties (e.g., distance with respect to objects, effective doorway widths, etc), but also considered strategic level. This comprised the result of for instance physiological processes or other (pyshological/sociological/physical). | This study assessed which simulation models are indeed capable of simulating pedestrian's strategic decisions.|
|Global route choice|Not every pedestrian has strict final destination: their destination might switch over time, therefore their global route might also change direction over time. The route that agents in a model follow towards their 'final destination' is determined by the presence of a global route choice algorithm.|An algorithm representing this specific choice behavior is either available or it is not.|
|Adaptive route choice| A model becomes more realistic when implementing adaptive route choice behavior| A model is assumed to implement adaptive route choice behavior when a local objective is present that causes local rerouting, for intance in case of congestion or high densities.|

- Social force model (SFM) was considered as continuous model with deterministic force-based interactions.
- The concept of SFM is based on the assumption that changes in the movement of pedestrians are influenced by social force field. 
- The result of the review indicated that three models (i.e., celullar automata that implement longer-range interactions, social force models, and nomad) are capable for reproducing the largest set of crowd motion phenomena
- Those three models can be indicated as the currently best crowd motion simulation models from a crowd movement phenomena point-of-view.
