## Boarding and alighting-related studies focusing policy intervention

- [Qu et al (2019)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physa.2019.121075) mentioned the categories of factors affecting the boarding and alighting process:
    - Train and platform facility environment: platform interface design, height difference between platform and vehicle floor, the door width, and stepping gap.
    - Human behavior: time pressure, boarding group size, boarding ratio, queuing discipline, competitive, and individual desired speed.

### Train facility environment
Focussing on train design implication on alighting and boarding dynamics:

- [Seriani and Fernandez, (2015)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2015.02.003) tested various scenarios, including the placement of **vertical handrail** and flow management of the passenger (specifically tested between **one-way door** separation between alighting and boarding, and **two-way door** which could be used for both alighting and boarding) with simulation and experimental tested which assessed by evaluation metrics, one of them is **Passenger Service Time (in sec)**.

- According to experimental result, [Seriani and Fernandez, (2015)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2015.02.003) suggested that one-way door is recommended for crowded station, the Passenger Service Time (PST) could be reduced by 31–82% compared to the use of two-way doors



### Platform facility environment

- Study from [Shen Y. et al (2026)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aap.2025.108388) developed models to simulate pedestrians’ movement focussing **on the platform** inside multilevel metro station.

- 



- The experiments from [Daamen et al, 2008](https://doi.org/10.3141/2042-08) tested the effects of the physical environment (stepping gap, height difference, and door width), population characteristics (age, gender), flow composition (direction, time pressure, luggage, boarding group size, boarding ratio, queuing discipline), and prevailing traffic conditions (crowding on board and crowding on the platform). 
- Mostly dwell time is consumed by boarding and alighting [Daamen et al, 2008](https://doi.org/10.3141/2042-08).
