## Terminology of disruptive event related to public transportation services and networks in academic literature

Recently, scholars have extensively examined events that might affected public transportation service and networks, by using various terminology such as: mega-events ([Parkes et.al.,2016](http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.tra.2016.07.006)), disruption/service disruption ([Pnevmatikou et.al,2015](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11116-015-9656-4); [Rahimi et.al.,2019](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trd.2019.10.011)) ~~large scale event~~. Below the synthesis of the terminology of such events in this study context:

- According to [Parkes et.al.,(2016)](http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.tra.2016.07.006), the term mega event refers to events that draw substantial numbers of individuals to a location, placing the local environment and infrastructure under great pressure, and bringing disruption to residents.


- According to [He et.al.,(2024)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tranpol.2024.02.008), scholars has categorized three main categories of transportation disruption: **planned-pre-announced, planned-unannounced and unplanned-unannounced**.
- Additionally, [He et.al.,(2024)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tranpol.2024.02.008) also stated that upgrades or maintenance works of transit infrastructure and special events (e.g., the Olympic games) belong to the first category (planned-pre-announced).

## Application of social force model in the rail transit services context
- [Yining Jia, et.al.,2026](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physa.2026.131279) proposed *Modified Social Force Model *(MSFM)* considering boarding alighting dynamics (passenger movement behavior) in metro transit system.
- MSFM incorporates eliptical agents, rotational dynamics, and specific DPE (Disembarking Preceed Embarking) rule.
- MSFM was based on realistic and controlled experimental phenomena, by incorporating DPE rule as well as unique behavior (i.e., following, avoiding and body rotation behavior).
