## Terminology of disruptive event related to public transportation services and networks in academic literature

Recently, scholars have extensively examined events that might affected public transportation service and networks, by using various terminology such as: mega-events ([Parkes et.al.,2016](http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.tra.2016.07.006)), disruption/service disruption ([Pnevmatikou et.al,2015](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11116-015-9656-4); [Rahimi et.al.,2019](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trd.2019.10.011)), shock to the transport system ([Jenelius E., and Mattsson, L.G.,2021](https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-08-102671-7.10719-5)), and large-scale event ([Kopsacheilis A., 2026](https://doi.org/10.1080/03081060.2026.2643729)). Below, the definition of such events proposed by scholars in academic literature:

- According to [Parkes et.al.,(2016)](http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.tra.2016.07.006), the term mega event refers to events that draw substantial numbers of individuals to a location, placing the local environment and infrastructure under great pressure, and bringing disruption to residents.

- According to [He S., et.al.,(2024)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tranpol.2024.02.008), scholars has categorized three main categories of transportation disruption: **planned-pre-announced, planned-unannounced and unplanned-unannounced**.
- Additionally, [He S., et.al.,(2024)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tranpol.2024.02.008) also stated that upgrades or maintenance works of transit infrastructure and special events (e.g., the Olympic games) belong to the first category (planned-pre-announced).

- On the other hand, below the transport disruption **typology** table developed by [Yusfida et.al.,(2026)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.urbmob.2026.100213).

| Group | Unplanned | Unplanned | Planned |
|:------|:----------|:----------|:--------|
|       | Natural | Unintentional Acts | Intentional Acts <mark>(generally preceded by pre-announcement)</marked> |
|Internal| Technical failure | Accident, handling error, negligence | Maintenance, transit strikes/ labour market disputes, transit construction/expansion
|External|Natural disasters (floods, earthquakes, storms, etc.), extreme weather,and pandemic| Accidents, negligence | Terrorism, acts of war, riots, criminal action, attacks, pranks, social movements

## Application of *social force model* (SFM) in the public transportation services context
### A modified social force model in boarding alighting process
- [Yining Jia, et.al.,2026](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physa.2026.131279) proposed *Modified Social Force Model (MSFM)* considering boarding alighting dynamics (passenger movement behavior) in metro transit system.
- Using video recording data to generate passenger walking trajectories by [Fu L., et.al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tust.2023.105362)
- MSFM incorporates eliptical agents, rotational dynamics, and specific DPE (Disembarking Preceed Embarking) rule.
- MSFM was based on realistic and controlled experimental phenomena, by incorporating DPE rule as well as unique behavior (i.e., following, avoiding, and body rotation behavior). 
- A new attraction force is proposed to MSFM ($f_{ig}$) to define behavior of following other pedestrians and avoiding obstacles.
- [Yining Jia, et.al.,2026](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physa.2026.131279) proposed <mark>resultant force</mark> (similar to pedestrian motivation) equation as follows (note: pedestrians are denoted by pedestrian $i$ and pedestrian $j$):


$$m_i\frac{dv_i}{dt}=f^0_i+\sum_{j(\neq i)}f_{ij}+\sum_{W}f_{iw}+f_{ig}$$

where attraction force $f_{ig}$ defined as:

$$f_{ig}=f_{i,m}\sum_{(j\neq i)}\beta_{ij1}\beta_{ij2}\beta_{ij3}\beta_{ij4}\beta_{ij5}\beta_{ij6}$$

where $\beta_{ijn}(n=1,2,3,4,5,6)$ are six dimensionless parameters and $f_{i,m}$ represents the order of attraction force. Below interpretation of six dimensionless parameters.

|Parameter|Definition|
|---------|----------|
|$\beta_{ij1}$| attraction force of pedestrian affected by distance, in terms of visual radius $(d_{ij})$ between pedestrian $i$ and pedestrian $j$ and visual radius $(l_{ij})$ of pedestrian $i$ |
|$\beta_{ij2}$| attraction force of pedestrian affected by instantaneous velocity of pedestrian $i$ $(v_i)$ and visual angle of the curent direction $(\pi)$|
|$\beta_{ij3}$| attraction force of pedestrian affected by the movement direction of pedestrian $i$ ($e_i^0$) and movement direction of pedestrian $j$ ($v_j$) as well as correlation of angle between $v_j$ and the target direction of pedestrian $i$ within less or equal to ${\pi}/{2}$|
|$\beta_{ij4}$| attraction force of pedestrian related to other pedestrian $j$'s speed $v_j$ (pedestrians facilitate a swift escape from walking predicaments by following individuals with higher movement speeds)|
|$\beta_{ij5}$| attraction force of pedestrian affected by distance between $i$ and $j$ ($d_{ij}$) also $r_i$ and $r_j$ (radius pedestrian $i$ and pedestrian $j$ respectively) -> important note $r_i$ + $r_j$ represents critical distance where pedestrians are in physical contact|
|$\beta_{ij6}$| attraction force of pedestrian affected by actual speed $(v_i)$ of pedestrian and desired speed $(v_i^0)$|

### Improved social force model in evacuation simulation under subway station fire incident
- [Zhang, et.al.,2023](https://doi.org/10.1080/17538947.2023.2197261) proposed an *Improved Social Force Model (ISFM)* based on crowd evacuation under subway <mark>station</mark> fire environments.
- Rationale of the study: addressing the usage of virtual-reality-based evacuation simulation to develop the ISFM, which can capture subway station fire incident situation.
- Data were generated by using virtual reality (VR) to depict the evacuation simulation in subway station.
- Accordingly, this study focused on the influence of "environmental role" and "subjective initiative" to improve original social force model.
- Below proposed model which take 'environmental role' and a 'subjective initiative' into account.
- Environmental role forces $(f_{ie})$ proposed as repulsive force, as in the original SFM, which can be described: 

$$f_{ie}= S \times exp \left(\frac{r_{ie}-d_{ie}}{B_e} \right)n_{ie}$$

where:
|Parameter|Description|
|---------|-----------|
|$S$|environmental role|
|$r_{ie}$|sum of the model radii of pedestrian $i$ to the model centre of the corresponding environment $e$|
|$d_{ie}$|distance from pedestrian $i$ to the model centre of the corresponding environment $e$|
|$B_e$|environmental influence range|
|$n_{ie}$|directional unit vector pointing from the corresponding environment $e$ to pedestrian $i$ with the direction of the force determined by the environmental action parameter $S$|


- In addition to ISFM, subjective initiative force ($f_{ig}$) also proposed which can be defined as subjective awareness of pedestrian.
- Subjective awareness considered several variables namely pedestrian level of fire safety knowledge ($k$), different level of familiarity with the subway station layout ($u$) and varying psychological conditions ($p$).
- The $f_{ig}$ can be expressed as follows:

$$f_{ig}= m_i \frac{v_i'e_i^{-0}-v_{i(t)}}{\tau_i}$$

where:
|Parameter|Description|
|---------|-----------|
|$m_i$|pedestrian's mass|
|$v_i$|initial desired rate of the pedestrian|
|$v_i'$|desired crowd evacuation speed|
|$e_i^{-0}$|desired pedestrian movement direction|
|$v_{i(t)}$|actual velocity|
|$\tau_{i}$|reaction time|
