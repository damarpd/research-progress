## Terminology of disruptive event related to public transportation services and networks in academic literature

Recently, scholars have extensively examined events that might affected public transportation service and networks, by using various terminology such as: mega-events ([Parkes et.al.,2016](http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.tra.2016.07.006)), disruption/service disruption ([Pnevmatikou et.al,2015](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11116-015-9656-4); [Rahimi et.al.,2019](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trd.2019.10.011)), shock to the transport system ([Jenelius E., and Mattsson, L.G.,2021](https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-08-102671-7.10719-5)). Below the synthesis of the terminology of such events in this study context:

- According to [Parkes et.al.,(2016)](http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.tra.2016.07.006), the term mega event refers to events that draw substantial numbers of individuals to a location, placing the local environment and infrastructure under great pressure, and bringing disruption to residents.


- According to [He et.al.,(2024)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tranpol.2024.02.008), scholars has categorized three main categories of transportation disruption: **planned-pre-announced, planned-unannounced and unplanned-unannounced**.
- Additionally, [He et.al.,(2024)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tranpol.2024.02.008) also stated that upgrades or maintenance works of transit infrastructure and special events (e.g., the Olympic games) belong to the first category (planned-pre-announced).

- On the other hand, below the transport disruption **typology** table proposed by [Yusfida et.al.,(2026)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.urbmob.2026.100213).

| Group | Unplanned | Unplanned | Planned |
|:------|:----------|:----------|:--------|
|       | Natural | Unintentional Acts | Intentional Acts <mark>(generally preceded by pre-announcement)</marked> |
|Internal| Technical failure | Accident, handling error, negligence | Maintenance, transit strikes/ labour market disputes, transit construction/expansion
|External|Natural disasters (floods, earthquakes, storms, etc.), extreme weather,and pandemic| Accidents, negligence | Terrorism, acts of war, riots, criminal action, attacks, pranks, social movements

## Application of social force model in the rail transit services context
### A modified social force model in boarding alighting process
- [Yining Jia, et.al.,2026](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physa.2026.131279) proposed *Modified Social Force Model (MSFM)* considering boarding alighting dynamics (passenger movement behavior) in metro transit system.
- MSFM incorporates eliptical agents, rotational dynamics, and specific DPE (Disembarking Preceed Embarking) rule.
- MSFM was based on realistic and controlled experimental phenomena, by incorporating DPE rule as well as unique behavior (i.e., following, avoiding, and body rotation behavior). 
- A new attraction force is introduced to MSFM ($f_{ij}$)

### Improved social force model 
- [Zhang, et.al.,2023](https://doi.org/10.1080/17538947.2023.2197261) proposed an *Improved Social Force Model (ISFM)* based on crowd evacuation under subway fire environments.
